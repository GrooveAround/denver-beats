# Denver Beats
## Your Guide to Live Music: May 29–June 5

---

**SHOW OF THE WEEK**

**My Morning Jacket, The Flaming Lips & Dawes** headline day two of Outside Days at Auraria Campus on May 30. This is the kind of lineup that reminds you why festival season exists—three generations of American rock weirdness on one bill, from MMJ's Southern-fried psych to the Lips' confetti-cannon chaos. Saturday's the strongest day of the festival by a mile.

---

**DON'T MISS THIS WEEK**

**Death Cab for Cutie** closes out Friday's Outside Days lineup (May 29) alongside Japanese Breakfast and Goth Babe. The fest is sold out, but if you snagged tickets months ago, this is your reminder that Ben Gibbard still writes the saddest songs about the Pacific Northwest. Auraria Campus transforms into one of Denver's best outdoor festival spaces for the weekend.

**Kesha** brings The Freedom Tour to Red Rocks on June 1 with Chromeo and Sizzy Rocket. Say what you will, but Kesha's live show has always been more fun than it has any right to be, and pairing her with Chromeo's electro-funk makes this feel like a 2 a.m. house party that somehow ended up at the best venue in America.

**Yo-Yo Ma with the Colorado Symphony** at Red Rocks (June 3) is the kind of show you tell people about for years. The world's most famous cellist performing on those rocks with a full orchestra—this transcends "concert" and becomes an actual event. Tickets are available through Ticketmaster.

**Dengue Fever** at Meow Wolf on May 29 is the perfect pairing of band and venue. Their Cambodian psych-rock suits the trippy art installation perfectly, and catching them in that space instead of a standard club feels like the show they were always meant to play. Bluebook opens.

**Burning Spear** hits both Cervantes (June 4, 16+) and Boulder Theater (June 5, all ages with adult) with Jesse Royal. At 78, Winston Rodney is reggae royalty, and these will likely be intimate shows by legend standards. The Boulder date is the all-ages option if you're bringing family.

**HIDDEN GEM**

**White Denim** at the Marquis on June 3 with Arc Iris. The Austin psych-rock trio has been one of America's tightest live bands for nearly two decades, burning through their catalog with the kind of musicianship that makes other guitarists quit. The Marquis is the right-sized room to feel the sweat.
